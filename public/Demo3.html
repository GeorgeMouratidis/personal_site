Computer Graphics and Virtual Realty

Project Description

This project consists of two parts: the creation of an indoor scene, and the mechanics of the player and objects. In the first part, after gathering and making the necessary object and texture resources, a home with four different rooms was created. This process required modelling (Blender) and UV mapping in order for the scene to be aesthetically pleasing. Furthermore a Phong lighting model was implemented, making use of ambient, diffuse and specular light, as well as shadowing so as to give a realistic aspect of the light. Finally, a collision detection algorithm between the player's camera and the rooms was implemented, to give a sense of realism.

The second part of the project, consists of the actual mechanics of the portals, which were implemented using the stencil buffer. The teleportation of the player includes the calculation of the camera's velocity and momentum vectors, as well as their respective angles and the camera's viewing angles. In adittion, a rigid body object in the shape of a sphere is implemented, which is also affected by gravity and collisions with the house's walls. The afforementioned object can also be thrown in a portal, in the same manner that a player can, and it would teleport to the portals exit.
