About Me

I am an Electrical and Computer Engineer graduate from the University of Patras in Greece. During my time as a student i have been intrigued by the fascinating world of physics and applied sciences as well as the important part of cumputer science in this field.
In the course of the University curricilum i have completed a plethora of demanding projects that have empowered my understanding of some of the most used programming languages such as C/C++, Python, SQL, as well as the functionality of concepts like 3D modelling, computanional geometry, computer graphics, UX/UI design and many more.

During my studies I have completed several diverse projects, showcasing my skills and expertise. These include developing a 3-dimensional object-to-object collision detection and voxel representation system. Additionally, I created an application that emulates the popular game "Portals" while incorporating real physics. In the context of database management, I designed and implemented a system for a bus company, streamlining operations and providing a user-friendly interface for itinerary-related inquiries. Furthermore, I applied UX/UI principles to redesign a bank's website, aiming to enhance the user experience. Complementing these practical projects, I have also conducted surveys and research on topics such as information retrieval and data mining, contributing to a well-rounded skill set and a deep understanding of diverse domains.

My diploma thesis with title "3D Reconstruction,Optimization,and AR Renderring", in principal consists of three seperate applications and combines the three scientific fields that i am most excited about. The first one takes as input a video recording of the user's room and extracts the camera's intrinsic parameters to generate as output a 3 dimensional representation of the room in the form of a point cloud.The second part of the application generates the optimal furniture layout using a genetic optimization algorithm based on an objective cost function. The output of this step is displayed in a graphical environment and consists of the optimal position and orientation of each furniture in the room.
The final part of the application helps the user visualize the optimal layout in their actual room,since each of the furniture is displayed in an augmented reality environment.
